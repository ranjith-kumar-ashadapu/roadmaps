PHASE 01
Foundation Boost
Weeks 1–3
⚡ Adapted for Math-Weak ProfileSkip epsilon-delta proofs. Focus on intuition: "what does a derivative tell me about a model?" Use 3Blue1Brown. Stop when you understand WHY, not HOW to prove it.

  • NumPy arrays & broadcasting
  • Pandas: groupby, merge, pivot
  • Matplotlib / Seaborn basics
  • Kaggle Titanic EDA practice
Week 2
  • Linear algebra intuition (vectors, dot product)
  • Stats: mean, variance, correlation, p-value
  • Apply stats on real datasets
Week 3
  • Calculus: derivatives as slopes, chain rule intuition
  • Probability: Bayes, distributions
  • SQL window functions review
  • Mini project: full EDA report

Insights
Bonus Sections
✕
TensorFlow 1.x / early TF 2.x — Dead in new projects. PyTorch dominates research + production in 2026. Do TF only if a specific job requires it.
✕
Kubernetes deep dive — You need "can deploy with K8s + minikube understanding." CKA certification is a DevOps career path, not ML engineer fresher.
✕
R programming — Python handles everything. R is niche (academia, biostat only). Zero ROI for your goals in 2026.
✕
MATLAB — Completely irrelevant in Python-first ML world. Nobody asks for it in industry JDs anymore.
✕
Full Apache Spark — PySpark basics is fine. Full Spark engineering is a Data Engineering track, not ML. Don't spend weeks here.
✕
Tutorial hell — Watching 50 videos without building. Max ratio: 1hr watching = 2hrs building. No exceptions.
✕
Learning LangChain + LlamaIndex together — Pick one. LlamaIndex is cleaner for RAG in 2026. Learn it deeply, not both shallowly.
#1 HOT
RAG + LlamaIndex/LangChain — Every company building internal AI tools needs this. 6-month demand trend still growing. Gets you freelance clients fast.
#2 HOT
AI Agents + Tool Calling — Agentic AI is the 2026 buzzword with real demand. LangGraph, CrewAI skills = premium ₹18–30 LPA range at startups.
#3 HOT
MLOps (FastAPI + Docker + MLflow) — ML engineers who can deploy are 40% rarer than those who only model. Instantly separates you in interviews.
#4 HOT
LoRA / QLoRA Fine-tuning — Domain-specific models are a goldmine for enterprises. Skill is accessible on Colab. High barrier in perception, low in reality.
#5 HOT
Multimodal AI (text + image) — GPT-4V, LLaVA, CLIP. Growing fast, few practitioners trained yet. Early mover advantage right now in 2026.

10 toy notebooks, 0 deployed demos — Recruiters open your GitHub, see 10 .ipynb files with no live links, and close. Every project needs a URL they can click.
AVOID
Ignoring math entirely — You need intuition, not proofs. 20% understanding = 80% ML productivity. Can you explain what gradient descent is doing? That's enough.
AVOID
Skipping GitHub commits — Every day without a commit is a missed social signal. Green contribution squares on GitHub are your silent resume before anyone reads a word.
AVOID
Applying too late — Start applying at Week 20 regardless of how ready you feel. The application + interview process itself teaches you what to study next.

The 72-Hour Reset ProtocolProcrastination hit and you fell off? This is the only protocol you need. Do not try to catch up. Do not restart from the beginning. Resume guilt kills more careers than missing days.
Day 1
Open GitHub. Make 1 tiny commit. Any file edit. Zero pressure. Just restore the habit of opening the laptop.
Day 2
Watch 1 video from exactly where you left off. Don't go back. Don't review. Forward only.
Day 3
Build something small. 30 minutes minimum. Commit it. You are now back on track. Restart here, not Week 1.
The Iron RuleNever miss two days in a row. Miss 1 day = fine, life happens. Miss 2 days = emergency reset protocol. Miss 3+ days = you've already started the protocol wrong. The streak is not about perfection. It's about never letting the gap grow.
